a week too late. but i say better late then never!


here's to my dearest little brother for completing his first 3 months of basic military training for his national service!
way to go!
my conclusion is that they really make a man out of you.
Boys who walked in 3 months ago come out looking like real man.
For his batch of recruits, their POP was held at the marina bay floating platform for the first time and the turn out was huge..
Personally, i am not a morning person at all and the ceremony was held so god damn early i felt like i was sleep walking my way to the bay.
We were actually there before 7am!!
Putting the fact that i was sleep deprived aside, i was actually very glad to be there to witness a rite of passage that my brother had to go through and i felt so proud of him!
Another reason was that the show was spectacular.
Dont get me wrong, the show i was referring to was happening at the stands. The POP was actually quite boring and the sun was searing us so badly that i wanted it to be over asap!!
My parents, Daphane and I were lucky to get one of the front row seats because we got there relatively early and we thought we were gonna get a good view of my brother in all his glory.
But this is where the show begins, ppl at the back were flocking to the front and standing at the railings to catch a better glimpse of there army boys creating this human wall that .... the view of the ppl sitting in their sits in front.(including us)
Of course the ppl in front started complaining to the soldier ushers in front but sadly, could not control the crowd and things started getting really ugly.
This women who was clad in very classy black dress took a piece of card/paper and started throwing them at the ppl sitting in front.
Like literally taking aim and firing at random ppl. Can you imagine how appaled i was?
At that moment, i really thought that she was "THE ugly singapore" personified, alive and breathing in front of me.
People started shouting at the crowd that was blocking us and more ppl joined in the fun and added to the chior of curses making the whole situation very uncomfortable.
My conclusion on this whole fiasco is that that soldiers should just stick to defending the country as they really did not do a very good job at crowd control.
I say if they had done a better job at making sure that ppl sat in their sits, this whole situation would not even have taken place.

So once again, congratulations my dear brother!
Maybe they should come up with a jingle to remind ppl to be nice at the POP next year.

Monday, July 19, 2010

When life gives you lemons, you make lemonades.

So this is the 3rd week of practicum and i think i am getting this teacher thing more and more.
like today, right now, at this very moment, i can say that i feel like a teacher.
It an incredible feeling, like power and control in the palm of your hands.
You blow a whistle and they stop, you say sit down and keep quite, they dont make a sound.
Awwweesome!!

For the first 2 weeks, i was feeling kinda awkward, a little out of place and confused
the role between a teacher and a student was kinda grey for me cause the last time i was in a school, i was being punished for not completing my tutorials and running into school cause im almost late for the morning assembly(although this is still happening)
i felt like a student to my CT but on the other hand, a teacher to the students in the school.
I guess you can not get anymore confused than this as you are taking on two roles that are polar opposite in nature like heaven and earth.

As i was saying i was feeling outta place so i had problems interacting with the students because you dont know if you want to take on the role of nice teacher that jokes with them or be the stern teacher that expects nonsense.
One student came up to me and asked:
"cher, can i go to the toilet? i wore my shorts inside out." (she really did)
I looked over to my CT and asked for permission and he said:
"hey jacq, you're a teacher yourself, come on man, you can do this"
So what i did basically was just.... ignore them altoghther and pretended that they were not there.

yeah.. outta sight outta mind they say.
they also say you live and you learn and i actually did learn ways to get yourself into the students' good books
Basically, you gotta get into their conversation
and how so you may say??
have you been in a situation where you realy want to join in a coversation but you just dont know how??
Well fret no more, cause here are tips and tricks for all you guys.
well, just 2, but 2 extremely tried and tested methods that really works

1. Make them feel uneasy
You make eye contact.
really uncomfortable eye contact.
let them feel uneasy and that you are coming

2. Personal space barrier
Break it!
You know how there is this imaginary line? cross it.
They will automatically acknowledge you and let you in the circle
and BAM! you're in

and voila!
you're officially one of them


p.s: they even did the chicken dance for me today after my lesson:)
